IBM Founder Thomas J. Watson Sr.

Thomas John Watson Sr. was one of the most successful and renowned entrepreneurs in the first half of 20th century .He was an American businessman who served as a chairman and CEO of IBM(International Business Machines Corporation).He was a pioneer in the development of computing and accounting equipment used in today's business ,science ,government and industry.
Born in February 17,1874(Campbell ,New York ,USA), Mr. Watson was an American industrialist who developed IBM into the largest manufacturer of electric typewriters and data processing equipments in the world. In every part of his life ,Mr. Watson continued having a deep interest in international relations. He adopted a slogan for IBM,"World peace through world trade" and closely worked with the Internatinal Chamber of Commerce. He supervised the company's growth into an International Force from 1914 to 1957. He served as a trustee of Columbia University and Lafayette college. Mr. Watson established a motto "Think" which later became a symbol of IBM.
